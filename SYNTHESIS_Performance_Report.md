## 1. Executive Summary
The SYNTHESIS model was developed to address the critical failures of standard meta-analysis methods (Fixed-Effect and REML Random-Effects) when faced with publication bias and small-study effects. While standard methods assume heterogeneity is random, SYNTHESIS treats it as a signal of potential "Bias" (systematic bias) or "Noise" data (noise).

In simulations with significant publication bias (small-study effects), standard **REML failed completely**, with coverage falling to **<5%** and bias exceeding **0.4 units**.

### Performance in Small Samples (k < 15)
Standard methods often become overconfident in small samples, producing narrow intervals that miss the true effect.
- **SYNTHESIS**'s "Density-based" function (Layer 2) and "Precision-based" weighting (Layer 3) ensure that precision is earned, not assumed. It showed the **lowest Mean Squared Error (MSE)** in stress tests.
